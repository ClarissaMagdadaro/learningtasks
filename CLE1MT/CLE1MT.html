WHAT IS CANCEL CULTURE

The notion of cancel culture is a variant of the term call-out culture. It is often said to take the form of boycotting or shunning an individual (often a celebrity) who is deemed to have acted or spoken in an unacceptable manner.

Cause

For some, it means an attack on free speech:

• It's an attempt to silence controversial or "politically incorrect" opinions.

• It makes people afraid to express their views without being shamed.

• It roots from the fact that people want to demand accountability from others

• It is caused by people who want to boycott others and are unapologetic towards others.

Effect

Others see it instead as consequence culture:

• It helps marginalized communities hold people accountable for causing harm.

• It teaches people to be more careful with their words and actions.

• It allows people to be responsible for their mistakes and educate themselves.

• Humans are flawed beings, and it’s in our nature to make mistakes. Their potentially permanent effects could leave everyone susceptible to being canceled.

Solution

Have your facts straight before you speak:

• Always be honest. The best defense is always the truth.

• Don’t try to cancel others. There’s no need to be cruel and adopt the tactics of cancellation. Be principled and don’t be a hypocrite.

• Don’t try to cancel others. There’s no need to be cruel and adapt the tactics of cancellation. Always practice proper netiquette and be respectful to everyone in the social media

Why we can’t stop fighting about cancel culture

Is cancel culture a mob mentality, or a long-overdue way of speaking truth to power? Some critics argue that cancel culture has a chilling effect on public discourse. Others argue that calls for "cancellation" are themselves a form of free speech and that they promote accountability. Some public figures claim to have been "canceled" while continuing their careers as before.
